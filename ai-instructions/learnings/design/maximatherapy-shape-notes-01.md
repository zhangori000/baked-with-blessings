> Notice the simplicity and almost "comic-san childness" that this site has.

Important findings:

1. The site’s decorative shapes are intentionally simple and toy-like, not realistic.
2. The cloud in the programs page is basically a row of overlapping horizontal ovals with no visible seams.
3. The middle cloud lobes are larger and taller than the side lobes.
4. The side lobes taper down rather than ending with a hard rectangular shoulder.
5. The cloud is allowed to extend off-screen, which helps it feel natural instead of centered and ornamental.
6. The sun is a plain filled circle inside a larger scalloped outline. It is just simple shapes stacked.
7. The butterfly is also just simple rounded lobes mirrored around a center body.
8. The white outline around those decorative shapes is thick and intentional, which keeps the forms readable against the cream page.
9. The page succeeds because the shapes are bold, filled, and unashamedly graphic.
10. There is no attempt to hide the fact that the forms are constructed from simple primitives.
11. The cream paper field stays broad and calm while the bright shapes do the expressive work.
12. This reference supports using overlapping ellipses for a cloud edge, but only if they overlap enough to read as one silhouette.
13. Visible gaps, straight seams, or repeated wave rhythms immediately break the effect.
14. If we adapt this language for Baked with Blessings, the right translation is “soft wool/cloud silhouette,” not “mathematical sine wave.”
15. Simplicity is doing the heavy lifting here. The shape system should be obvious, clean, and playful, not fussy.
