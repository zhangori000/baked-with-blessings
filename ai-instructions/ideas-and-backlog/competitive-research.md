# Competitive Research & Inspiration

A place to dump observations from other food/drink ordering sites.

## 2026-04-10 — First pass

Looked at: Starbucks, McDonald's (via DoorDash), DoorDash home, Crumbl Cookies

### Starbucks (starbucks.com/menu)
- Left sidebar with drink categories (Protein Beverages, Hot Coffee, Cold Coffee, Matcha, etc.) + Food section
- Main area shows circular product images with category names
- "Trending" and "Energy Refreshers" featured at top
- Clean but A LOT of categories — long sidebar scroll

### McDonald's (mcdonalds.order.online — powered by DoorDash)
- Horizontal tab navigation: Most Ordered, Limited Time, Extra Value Meals, Burgers, Chicken, etc.
- Product cards with image, name, price, like %, and number of ratings
- "Most liked" badges on popular items
- Plus button on each card for quick add

### DoorDash (doordash.com/home)
- Delivery/Pickup toggle top right
- Left sidebar: Grocery, Retail, Catering, Reservations, Late Night, Alcohol, Apparel, etc.
- Weekly Deals carousel with restaurant cards (rating, delivery time, delivery fee, promo)
- Feels like a LOT going on — many categories, many promos

### Crumbl Cookies (crumblcookies.com)
- Pink branding, very clean and focused
- Login via phone number (SMS code)
- Personalized: "Welcome back, Orien" with rewards (Crumbs points)
- Simple product grid: Single, 4-Pack, 6-Pack, 12-Pack with save % badges
- Mini Desserts section below
- Much simpler than the others — fewer choices, more focused

### Overall takeaway
- As a customer, sites like Starbucks and DoorDash feel like TOO MUCH STUFF
- Crumbl's focused, simple approach feels nicer — fewer decisions, cleaner layout
- Something to think about for Baked with Blessings' menu/ordering UX
