# downside

The downside of all the examples in ai-instructions/design-stuff is that they're far too over-organized, and overwhelming to the user. When a person goes to a real bakery or coffee shop, they like to see the windowed display in front of them with "grids" of products.

Likewise, I want to split up my products in categories, but each cateogry is a CONTIGUOUS , slightly-adjacent GRID, with smaller image sizes. We scroll horizontally if there are a lot of items per cateogory (fits for mobile view too). clicking a grid item will expand that grid item into some product modal, there is a modal to go to the REAL complete product page, but you could also just see descirption, comparisons, add to cart, and more, just through that pop up with animation too.
