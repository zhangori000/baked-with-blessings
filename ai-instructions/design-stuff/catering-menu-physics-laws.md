# Catering Menu Physics Laws

These laws take precedence for the catering menu decorative system.

1. Clouds are made from tightly packed opaque circles only.
2. Clouds must not have visible white holes or negative-space gaps in their middle.
3. Adjacent cloud circles should overlap heavily; target overlap is roughly 75% of each circle's area.
4. Cloud silhouettes should stay close to an overall oval or circular shape. Avoid croissant, crescent, or arched-rainbow cloud shapes.
5. No flat rounded-rectangle cloud bases unless explicitly requested again.
6. Grass must visually connect directly to the blue sky or sky-toned band with no cream strip interrupting it.
7. Flowers must look planted: stems grow out of grass or progress bars, not float above them.
8. Decorative motion should read like growth or bobbing, not flying away or drifting off-screen.
