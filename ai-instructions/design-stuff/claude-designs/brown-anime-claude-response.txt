## Color palette reference

If you want to recreate the mood elsewhere, these are the exact colors used throughout:

**Sky gradient:** `#7cc3e8` → `#c5dff0` → `#f5d5d8` → `#f8e4c8`
**Cloud pink underside:** `#f5d5d8` and `#e8c4d0`
**Meadow gradient:** `#f4d96b` → `#e8c547` → `#d4a73a` → `#9c7a2e`
**Grass greens:** `#4a6b28` (dark), `#558b2f` (mid), `#6b8534` (light)
**Dry grass/reeds:** `#8a7a2a`, `#8b7355`, `#d4a574`, `#c49464`
**Pink florals:** `#e8a8c8` (light), `#d48db5` (mid), `#c9588f` (deep), `#8b3a62` (center), `#7a2848` (darkest center)

Each component is positioned relative to its own viewBox, so you can place them freely. The flowers use negative coordinates in their viewBox so the bloom sits at `(0, 0)` — that makes it easy to drop multiples at different `translate()` positions without math.
