Create mobile-first SVG backdrop variants for the Baked With Blessings scenery system.

Goal:
These are NOT full scenes with every flower/cloud/foreground embellishment. They are backdrop-focused mobile variants meant to preserve the focal subject on narrow screens. Desktop versions already exist. Mobile versions should solve cropping/composition problems on phones.

Scenes needed:
1. `blossom`
2. `moonlit`
3. `fairy-castle`

Output format:
- One SVG per scene.
- Keep each SVG self-contained.
- Match the current visual language of the existing desktop scene.
- Use clean, organized SVG structure with groups/comments if helpful.
- Use `viewBox` values designed for portrait mobile, not landscape desktop.
- Do not include external assets.

Important constraints:
- Do NOT spend effort on decorative flower rails, spawned flowers, rabbits, sheep, or button chrome.
- Do NOT include extra floating cloud systems unless the backdrop itself truly needs them.
- Focus on the background composition only: sky, landforms, tree/castle/moon, large scene silhouette, and any absolutely essential atmospheric forms.
- These should work well as `object-fit: cover` backgrounds inside a tall mobile hero.

Scene-specific art direction:

1. Blossom mobile backdrop
- The focal point must be the cherry blossom tree.
- The tree should be clearly visible on mobile without getting cropped into abstract pink circles.
- Favor a composition where the trunk and main canopy mass are readable.
- Keep a soft blue sky and a small amount of drifting petal atmosphere.
- The open sky area should still leave room for menu text/buttons on the upper-left/mid-left.
- Avoid excessive zoom. The current problem is that mobile crops too tightly and loses the tree structure.

2. Moonlit mobile backdrop
- The focal point must be the moon and moon-glow.
- The moon must remain visible on mobile.
- Keep the dreamy dark-to-teal gradient and a restrained atmospheric feel.
- If there are cloud forms, they should not cover the moon or make the top-left unreadable.
- Preserve the aurora/glow feeling.
- Mobile composition should intentionally place the moon where it survives narrow cropping.

3. Fairy-castle mobile backdrop
- This should be more zoomed out than the current mobile crop.
- The castle must read as a full fairy-tale landmark, not just a cropped central tower.
- Maintain the layered mountain/mist valley mood.
- Keep the composition tall enough that the castle sits within a readable landscape context.
- Preserve the “storybook valley” feel, but simplify if needed for mobile clarity.

Technical guidance:
- Design these as portrait-first mobile backdrops, roughly conceptually similar to a 9:16 stage.
- Leave clean negative space where the UI text/buttons can sit.
- Avoid too much tiny detail that will disappear on phones.
- Large shapes and clear silhouettes matter more than dense decoration.

Suggested filenames:
- `blossom-breeze-sky-mobile.svg`
- `moonlit-purple-sky-mobile.svg`
- `fairy-castle-mobile.svg`

What success looks like:
- On a phone, blossom clearly shows a tree.
- On a phone, moonlit clearly shows the moon.
- On a phone, fairy-castle feels zoomed out and readable.
- The SVGs feel like mobile companions to the existing desktop backdrops, not unrelated redraws.
