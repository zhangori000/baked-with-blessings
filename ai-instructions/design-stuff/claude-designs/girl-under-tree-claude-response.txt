Key changes from facing-forward to facing-away:

The **hat** now has its dome centered rather than tilted in profile — both sides of the brim are equally visible, with the band ribbon's knot peeking out in the back center. No bangs or face-framing hair since the brim itself hides the head.

The **hair** cascades symmetrically down the back in a wide curtain, hiding most of the upper body. I added subtle darker strands as highlights for texture. The tips are slightly wavy where they end.

The **dress** and **sleeves** read the same from behind, but the **red sash** now shows a proper **bow with two loops, a center knot, and trailing ribbons** — the big visual payoff of the back view, since that's where the bow would actually be tied.

The **skirt** has center-fold shading instead of the profile silhouette, and the hem pattern wraps continuously across the full width.

A tiny bit of **neck** shows between the hair mass and the hat brim to avoid the "floating hat" look.
