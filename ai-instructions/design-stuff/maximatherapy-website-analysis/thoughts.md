Notice the simplicity and almost "comic-san childness" that this site has.

The home page animation is useful because it is not just one static composition. `home-page-frame-1.png`, `home-page-frame-2.png`, and `home-page-frame-3.png` show the same light background while the white cloud shapes drift from left to right. The clouds are simple, chunky white silhouettes, so the motion reads clearly even though the forms are minimal.

`home-page-frame-4.png` and `home-page-frame-5.png` show the next background after clicking the right-arrow button. The background changes, and the new clouds are a different family of shapes: three "half-egg" forms, split in the vertical direction, moving from right to left. That change matters because the site is not reusing one cloud asset everywhere. Each page state gets its own shape language.

`home-page-frame-6.png` and `home-page-frame-7.png` show another right-arrow transition into the purple theme. The clouds again move from right to left, but this time they look like two rectangles stacked on each other with very aggressive border radii. The lesson is that the animation system keeps the same idea of drifting white forms while letting the silhouette itself change with the new page color story.

`home-page-scroll-1.png` into `home-page-scroll-2.png` is also important. Once the page scrolls down, the background becomes a lighter, softer color field and more clouds continue floating through the composition. The clouds are not only for the hero state; they keep participating in the page as the user moves downward, which helps the whole site feel like one continuous animated environment instead of a single decorated header.
